## What is a Mini PC? {#what-is-mini-pc}

A **mini PC** (also called a small form factor PC or compact desktop) is a fully functional computer in a significantly smaller chassis than traditional desktop towers. Typically measuring under 2 liters in volume, mini PCs deliver desktop-class performance while occupying minimal desk space.

### Mini PC vs Traditional Desktop

| Feature | Mini PC | Traditional Desktop |
|---------|---------|---------------------|
| **Size** | 0.5-2L volume | 15-40L volume |
| **Weight** | 0.3-1.5 kg | 5-15 kg |
| **Power consumption** | 15-65W typical | 200-500W typical |
| **Upgradeability** | Limited | Extensive |
| **Portability** | Easy to relocate | Stationary |
| **Noise level** | Quiet to silent | Moderate to loud |
| **Price range** | $150-$1,500 | $300-$3,000+ |

### Why Mini PCs Are Gaining B2B Traction

**1. Space Efficiency**
- 90% smaller footprint than traditional desktops
- Ideal for space-constrained environments
- VESA mountable behind monitors

**2. Energy Savings**
- 60-80% lower power consumption
- Reduced cooling requirements
- Lower electricity costs at scale

**3. Deployment Flexibility**
- Easy to ship and install
- Quick replacement and rotation
- Suitable for distributed locations

**4. Total Cost of Ownership**
- Lower hardware costs
- Reduced energy bills
- Simplified logistics

For businesses deploying 100+ units, mini PCs can reduce total cost of ownership by 25-40% compared to traditional desktops.

## Mini PC Types and Form Factors {#mini-pc-types}

Understanding mini PC categories helps you match products to specific use cases.

### By Size Category

#### Ultra-Compact (Under 0.5L)
**Examples:** Intel NUC, AIERXUAN DU Series

**Characteristics:**
- Smallest form factor
- Fanless options available
- Limited expansion
- Best for: Digital signage, thin client, kiosk

**Typical specs:**
- Intel N100/N200 or Core i3
- 8-16GB RAM
- 256-512GB SSD
- 1-2 USB ports, HDMI

#### Compact (0.5-1L)
**Examples:** AIERXUAN Mini Series, Lenovo ThinkCentre Tiny

**Characteristics:**
- Balance of size and performance
- Active cooling
- Some expansion options
- Best for: Office productivity, POS systems

**Typical specs:**
- Intel Core i5/i7 or AMD Ryzen 5/7
- 16-32GB RAM
- 512GB-1TB SSD
- Multiple USB, dual display support

#### Performance (1-2L)
**Examples:** AIERXUAN Gaming Mini, Mac Mini

**Characteristics:**
- Higher performance ceiling
- Dedicated GPU options
- Better thermal management
- Best for: Content creation, gaming, workstation

**Typical specs:**
- Intel Core i7/i9 or AMD Ryzen 7/9
- 32-64GB RAM
- 1-2TB NVMe SSD
- Thunderbolt, multiple displays

### By Cooling Type

#### Fanless (Passive Cooling)
**Advantages:**
- Zero noise operation
- No moving parts (higher reliability)
- Dust-resistant
- Ideal for: Healthcare, recording studios, retail

**Limitations:**
- Lower performance ceiling
- Higher surface temperature
- Limited to low-TDP processors

#### Active Cooling (Fan-based)
**Advantages:**
- Higher performance potential
- Better sustained workloads
- More configuration options

**Limitations:**
- Some noise generation
- Fan maintenance required
- Dust accumulation over time

## Key Specifications Explained {#specifications}

### Processor (CPU)

The CPU determines overall system performance. For B2B applications:

**Entry-Level (Intel N100/N200, Celeron)**
- Web browsing, email, basic office
- Digital signage, thin clients
- Price point: $150-$300

**Mainstream (Intel Core i5, AMD Ryzen 5)**
- Full office productivity
- Video conferencing
- Light multitasking
- Price point: $350-$600

**Performance (Intel Core i7/i9, AMD Ryzen 7/9)**
- Heavy multitasking
- Content creation
- Software development
- Price point: $600-$1,200+

**Key CPU considerations:**
- **Generation matters:** 13th/14th Gen Intel or Ryzen 7000/8000 series
- **TDP (Thermal Design Power):** Higher TDP = more performance but more heat
- **vPro support:** Essential for enterprise management

### Memory (RAM)

| RAM | Suitable For | Notes |
|-----|--------------|-------|
| 8GB | Basic productivity | Minimum for Windows 11 |
| 16GB | Business standard | Recommended baseline |
| 32GB | Power users, VMs | Future-proof option |
| 64GB | Workstation tasks | Specialized needs |

**RAM considerations for B2B:**
- **Upgradeable vs. soldered:** Upgradeable allows future expansion
- **DDR4 vs. DDR5:** DDR5 offers 50% more bandwidth
- **Dual-channel:** Always prefer dual-channel for better performance

### Storage

| Type | Speed | Best For | Price/GB |
|------|-------|----------|----------|
| SATA SSD | 500 MB/s | Budget builds | $0.08-0.10 |
| NVMe Gen3 | 3,500 MB/s | Mainstream | $0.10-0.12 |
| NVMe Gen4 | 7,000 MB/s | Performance | $0.12-0.15 |

**Storage recommendations by use case:**
- **Digital signage:** 128-256GB SATA SSD
- **Office productivity:** 256-512GB NVMe
- **Content creation:** 1TB+ NVMe Gen4
- **Data processing:** Dual drive configuration

### Connectivity

**Essential ports for business:**
- USB-A 3.0/3.2 (2-4 ports minimum)
- USB-C (preferably with Power Delivery)
- HDMI 2.0 or DisplayPort (dual display support)
- Gigabit Ethernet (essential for enterprise)
- Audio jack

**Optional but valuable:**
- Thunderbolt 4 (for docking, high-speed peripherals)
- SD card reader
- Serial/COM port (industrial applications)
- WiFi 6E / Bluetooth 5.2

### Display Output

| Output | Resolution Support | Use Case |
|--------|-------------------|----------|
| HDMI 1.4 | Up to 4K@30Hz | Basic displays |
| HDMI 2.0 | Up to 4K@60Hz | Standard business |
| DisplayPort 1.4 | Up to 8K@60Hz | High-resolution |
| Thunderbolt 4 | Up to 8K@60Hz | Premium setups |

**Multi-display considerations:**
- Most mini PCs support 2-3 displays
- Check maximum resolution per output
- Verify simultaneous display support
