## What is Clevo? Understanding the Barebone Model {#what-is-clevo}

**Clevo** is a Taiwan-based ODM (Original Design Manufacturer) that specializes in barebone laptop chassis—essentially laptop shells with motherboards, displays, and cooling systems, but without RAM, storage, or operating systems installed.

### How the Clevo Model Works

Unlike traditional laptop brands that sell complete, ready-to-use systems, Clevo sells semi-finished products to resellers who then:

1. **Purchase barebone chassis** from Clevo distributors
2. **Add components** (RAM, SSD, WiFi card, OS)
3. **Apply their own branding** (stickers, boot logos, packaging)
4. **Sell to end customers** under their brand name

**Key Clevo Characteristics:**

| Aspect | Clevo Approach |
|--------|----------------|
| Product Type | Barebone chassis (semi-finished) |
| Customization | Component selection only |
| Branding | Limited (stickers, software) |
| MOQ | As low as 1 unit |
| Design Ownership | Clevo owns all designs |
| Target Market | Gaming, workstation, enthusiast |

### Popular Clevo Laptop Series

Clevo organizes products by chassis code, with resellers often renaming them:

- **P-Series**: High-performance gaming (17.3", desktop-class GPUs)
- **N-Series**: Mainstream gaming and productivity
- **W-Series**: Mobile workstations
- **L-Series**: Lightweight and ultraportable

According to [NotebookReview's Clevo Guide](http://www.notebookreview.com/news/clevo-guide/), Clevo chassis are used by over 50 reseller brands worldwide, including Sager (US), XMG (Germany), and Metabox (Australia).

## Clevo vs Traditional OEM Manufacturing {#clevo-vs-oem}

Understanding the difference between Clevo's ODM model and traditional OEM partnerships is crucial for making the right sourcing decision.

### The Fundamental Difference

| Factor | Clevo (ODM Reselling) | Traditional OEM |
|--------|----------------------|-----------------|
| **Design Ownership** | Clevo owns design | You own design |
| **Chassis Customization** | None (use existing) | Full custom possible |
| **Branding Depth** | Surface-level only | Complete (chassis, BIOS, packaging) |
| **MOQ** | 1-10 units | 100-1,000+ units |
| **Exclusivity** | Zero (same chassis sold to competitors) | Can be 100% exclusive |
| **Lead Time** | 1-2 weeks | 4-12 weeks |
| **Unit Cost** | Higher per-unit | Lower at volume |

### What "OEM Laptop" Really Means

When buyers search for "OEM laptops," they're often looking for one of two things:

1. **Clevo-style reselling**: Buying existing designs and adding components
2. **True OEM manufacturing**: Having laptops built to their specifications

For a detailed explanation of OEM manufacturing fundamentals, see our [Complete Guide to OEM Manufacturing](/en/blog/what-is-oem-manufacturing-complete-explanation-b2b-buyers).

### The Hidden Cost of Non-Exclusivity

One critical factor Clevo resellers often overlook: **your competitors can sell the identical chassis**.

A 2024 analysis of gaming laptop brands revealed that 23 different companies sold laptops based on the Clevo NH70 chassis in the US market alone. This creates:

- **Price race to bottom**: Competitors undercut on identical hardware
- **No differentiation**: Customers compare only on price and service
- **Brand confusion**: Customers recognize the chassis, not your brand

## Who Uses Clevo Laptops? {#who-uses-clevo}

Clevo's barebone model serves specific market segments effectively:

### Ideal Clevo Use Cases

**1. Gaming PC Builders & Enthusiast Shops**
- Low volume (10-100 units/month)
- Customers want specific component configurations
- Technical expertise to assemble and support
- Example: Boutique gaming PC shops

**2. System Integrators**
- Need high-performance workstations
- Government/enterprise contracts requiring specific specs
- Can handle component installation
- Example: CAD workstation providers

**3. Regional Resellers**
- Serving local markets with limited volume
- Want gaming laptops without major brand relationships
- Example: Country-specific gaming brands

### Where Clevo Falls Short

**1. Volume Buyers (500+ units)**
- Per-unit costs become uncompetitive
- No volume discounts on chassis
- Assembly labor adds significant cost

**2. Brand Builders**
- Cannot differentiate on hardware design
- Limited branding options
- Customers may recognize Clevo chassis

**3. Business/Education Markets**
- Clevo focuses on gaming/workstation
- Limited thin-and-light options
- No business-specific features (vPro, smart card)

## Cost Analysis: Clevo Reselling vs OEM Partnership {#cost-analysis}

Let's compare real costs for a mid-range gaming laptop (15.6", RTX 4060, i7-13700H):

### Clevo Reselling Cost Structure

| Cost Component | Amount (USD) | Notes |
|----------------|--------------|-------|
| Clevo barebone chassis | $650-750 | NH55 or similar |
| RAM (32GB DDR5) | $80-100 | Retail pricing |
| SSD (1TB NVMe) | $60-80 | Retail pricing |
| WiFi card | $15-25 | If not included |
| Windows license | $100-140 | OEM license |
| Assembly labor | $30-50 | Per unit |
| Packaging/branding | $10-20 | Basic |
| **Total Cost** | **$945-1,165** | Per unit |

### OEM Manufacturing Cost Structure (500 units)

| Cost Component | Amount (USD) | Notes |
|----------------|--------------|-------|
| Complete laptop (FOB) | $680-750 | Fully assembled |
| Custom branding | Included | Logo, BIOS, packaging |
| Windows license | $80-100 | Volume licensing |
| Shipping (sea) | $15-25 | Per unit to US |
| **Total Cost** | **$775-875** | Per unit |

### Break-Even Analysis

| Order Volume | Clevo Cost/Unit | OEM Cost/Unit | Savings with OEM |
|--------------|-----------------|---------------|------------------|
| 50 units | $1,050 | $950* | -$100 (OEM higher) |
| 100 units | $1,050 | $875 | $175 (17%) |
| 500 units | $1,050 | $800 | $250 (24%) |
| 1,000 units | $1,050 | $750 | $300 (29%) |

*Note: OEM costs at 50 units include higher per-unit overhead

**Key Insight**: At volumes above 100 units, OEM manufacturing typically delivers 15-30% cost savings compared to Clevo reselling, while providing full branding control.

## When to Choose Clevo vs Custom OEM {#when-to-choose}

### Choose Clevo When:

✅ **Volume under 50 units/month**
- OEM setup costs don't justify at low volume
- Clevo's flexibility suits small batches

✅ **Need immediate availability**
- Clevo chassis ship within days
- No production lead time

✅ **Targeting enthusiast/gaming niche**
- Customers value raw specs over brand
- Technical buyers appreciate component choice

✅ **Testing market demand**
- Low-risk way to enter laptop market
- Validate demand before OEM investment

### Choose OEM Manufacturing When:

✅ **Volume exceeds 100 units/order**
- Cost savings justify OEM relationship
- Economies of scale kick in

✅ **Brand differentiation matters**
- Custom chassis design possible
- Exclusive product lineup

✅ **Targeting business/education markets**
- Need specific certifications (EPEAT, TCO)
- Business features (vPro, fingerprint, smart card)

✅ **Building long-term brand**
- Own your product design
- Competitors can't copy your hardware

```

  │
  ├─ Need custom chassis design?

  │   └─ NO ↓
  │
  ├─ Targeting gaming/enthusiast market?
  │   └─ YES → Clevo may work

      └─ YES → OEM Manufacturing
```

## Frequently Asked Questions {#faq}

### What does Clevo stand for?

Clevo is a brand name, not an acronym. The company was founded in Taiwan in 1983 and has become one of the world's largest ODM manufacturers of gaming and workstation laptops. The name doesn't have a specific meaning—it's simply the company's trade name.

### Are Clevo laptops good quality?

Clevo laptops are generally well-built with quality components, particularly for gaming and workstation use. However, quality perception depends on the reseller—since Clevo sells barebone chassis, the final product quality depends on component selection, assembly quality, and support provided by the reseller. Major Clevo resellers like Sager and XMG have strong reputations for quality.

### Why are Clevo laptops cheaper than major brands?

Clevo laptops cost less because:
1. **No marketing overhead**: Clevo doesn't advertise to consumers
2. **Reseller model**: Lower distribution costs
3. **Component flexibility**: Buyers choose their own RAM/storage
4. **No brand premium**: You're paying for hardware, not brand name

However, this comes with trade-offs: limited warranty support, no retail presence, and less polished software experience.

### Can I put my own brand on Clevo laptops?

Yes, but with limitations. Clevo resellers can add:
- Custom boot logos and BIOS splash screens
- Branded stickers and labels
- Custom packaging
- Pre-installed software

However, you cannot modify the chassis design, keyboard layout, or port configuration. For deeper branding, you need a true OEM partnership.

### What is the minimum order for OEM laptops?

MOQ varies significantly by manufacturer:
- **Clevo reselling**: 1 unit (but no customization)
- **Flexible OEMs (AIERXUAN)**: 100 units
- **Mid-tier ODMs**: 500-1,000 units
- **Tier-1 ODMs (Quanta, Compal)**: 10,000+ units

For most B2B buyers, the 100-500 unit range offers the best balance of customization and cost efficiency.

### How do I become a Clevo reseller?

To resell Clevo laptops:
1. Contact a Clevo distributor in your region
2. Apply for a reseller account (may require business documentation)
3. Meet minimum purchase requirements (varies by distributor)
4. Set up assembly capabilities or partner with an assembler

Major Clevo distributors include Sager (US), BTO (Netherlands), and various regional partners.
