## Quick Start: Is Custom Manufacturing Right for You? {#quick-start}

Before diving in, assess if custom laptop manufacturing fits your situation:

| Factor | Custom Manufacturing | Retail/Reseller |
|--------|---------------------|-----------------|
| Order volume | 100+ units | Any quantity |
| Timeline | 6-16 weeks | Immediate |
| Unit cost | 30-45% lower | Retail price |
| Branding | Full control | None |
| Specifications | You define | Fixed options |
| Upfront investment | Medium-High | Low |

**Custom manufacturing makes sense when:**
- You need 100+ units with consistent specifications
- Brand identity matters to your business
- You want specifications not available retail
- You're building a recurring product line

**Stick with retail/reseller when:**
- You need fewer than 50 units
- Timeline is under 4 weeks
- One-time purchase with no repeat orders

## Step 4: Negotiate Terms and Contracts {#step-4-contracts}

This step is often overlooked but critical for project success.

### Payment Terms

**Standard payment structure:**

| Milestone | Percentage | When |
|-----------|------------|------|
| Deposit | 30% | Order confirmation |
| Pre-production | 40% | Before mass production starts |
| Balance | 30% | Before shipping (or against B/L) |

**Negotiation tips:**
- First order: 30/40/30 is standard, don't push too hard
- Repeat orders: Negotiate to 30/70 or even 20/80
- Large orders (1000+): May get 20/30/50 or letter of credit terms

### Key Contract Clauses

**Must-have clauses:**

1. **Specification lock:** Exact components with part numbers
2. **Quality standards:** AQL levels, testing requirements
3. **Delivery schedule:** Penalties for delays (typically 1-2% per week, capped at 10%)
4. **Warranty terms:**
   - Standard: 12 months parts replacement
   - DOA allowance: 1-2% acceptable
   - RMA process and timeline
5. **IP protection:** NDA, design ownership, non-compete

**Sample warranty clause:**
> "Manufacturer warrants products against defects in materials and workmanship for 12 months from delivery date. DOA (Dead on Arrival) rate not to exceed 1.5%. Manufacturer shall replace defective units within 30 days of RMA approval at no cost to Buyer."

### Pricing Negotiation

| Tactic | Potential Savings |
|--------|-------------------|
| Commit to annual volume | 5-10% |
| Pay faster (net 15 vs net 30) | 2-3% |
| Accept previous-gen CPU | 15-25% |
| Standardize to one configuration | 3-5% |
| Order during off-peak (Feb-Apr) | 5-8% |

## Step 5: Prototype and Testing {#step-5-prototype}

Never skip this step. Issues found in prototyping cost 10x less to fix than in production.

### Prototype Stages

| Stage | Purpose | Units | Timeline | Cost |
|-------|---------|-------|----------|------|
| EVT (Engineering) | Verify functionality | 3-5 | 2-4 weeks | $1,500-3,000 |
| DVT (Design) | Validate design, stress test | 10-20 | 2-4 weeks | $5,000-10,000 |
| PVT (Production) | Verify production line | 50-100 | 1-2 weeks | At unit cost |

### Testing Checklist

**Functional (all units):**
- [ ] Boot test, BIOS access
- [ ] All ports functional
- [ ] Display: no dead pixels, uniform brightness
- [ ] Keyboard: all keys responsive
- [ ] Trackpad: gesture support, accuracy
- [ ] Audio: speakers, microphone, headphone jack
- [ ] Camera: image quality, privacy shutter
- [ ] WiFi/Bluetooth: connection stability
- [ ] Battery: charges to 100%, runtime test

**Stress tests (sample units):**
- [ ] Thermal: CPU temp under sustained load (<95°C)
- [ ] Battery: 500 cycle degradation test
- [ ] Hinge: 20,000 open/close cycles
- [ ] Keyboard: 10 million keystroke test
- [ ] Drop: 76cm drop test (MIL-STD-810G)

**Compliance (certification samples):**
- [ ] FCC Part 15 (USA)
- [ ] CE marking (EU)
- [ ] RoHS compliance
- [ ] Energy Star (if applicable)

## Step 6: Production and Delivery {#step-6-production}

### Production Timeline

| Phase | Duration | Your Actions |
|-------|----------|--------------|
| Component procurement | 2-4 weeks | Confirm component availability |
| Production | 2-4 weeks | Request daily/weekly updates |
| Quality inspection | 3-5 days | Arrange third-party inspection |
| Packaging | 2-3 days | Approve packaging samples |
| Shipping (sea) | 3-5 weeks | Arrange customs clearance |
| **Total** | **10-16 weeks** | |

### Quality Control

**Recommended AQL (Acceptable Quality Level):**
- Critical defects (safety, non-functional): 0%
- Major defects (significant function issues): 1.0%
- Minor defects (cosmetic): 2.5%

**Third-party inspection options:**
- SGS, Bureau Veritas, Intertek: $300-500 per inspection
- Freelance inspectors: $150-250 per day

### Shipping Options

| Method | Transit Time | Cost/Unit* | Best For |
|--------|--------------|------------|----------|
| Sea (FCL) | 25-35 days | $3-5 | 500+ units |
| Sea (LCL) | 30-40 days | $8-12 | 100-500 units |
| Air freight | 5-7 days | $20-35 | Urgent, <200 units |
| Express (DHL/FedEx) | 3-5 days | $40-60 | Samples, <50 units |

*Approximate cost China to US West Coast

## Case Study: 500-Unit Education Project {#case-study}

**Client:** Regional education technology company (name withheld)
**Goal:** Custom-branded laptops for K-12 classroom deployment

### Requirements
- Budget: $420/unit manufacturing cost
- Specs: Intel i3, 8GB RAM, 256GB SSD, 14" display
- Features: Spill-resistant keyboard, reinforced hinges, asset tag slot
- Branding: Custom boot logo, keyboard logo, packaging
- Timeline: 10 weeks from order to delivery

### Process Timeline

| Week | Activity | Outcome |
|------|----------|---------|
| 1-2 | RFQ to 6 manufacturers | 4 qualified responses |
| 3 | Sample evaluation | Selected manufacturer |
| 4-5 | EVT prototypes | 2 design issues identified |
| 6-7 | DVT prototypes | Issues resolved, approved |
| 8-11 | Production | 500 units completed |
| 12-13 | Shipping + customs | Delivered |

### Results
- **Final cost:** $408/unit (3% under budget)
- **Quality:** 0.4% DOA rate (2 units)
- **Timeline:** 13 weeks (3 weeks over initial target due to EVT issues)

### Lessons Learned
1. **Budget buffer:** Initial EVT issues required chassis modification—always budget 10-15% contingency
2. **Prototype time:** Don't rush EVT/DVT—the 2 extra weeks saved potential production disaster
3. **Communication:** Weekly video calls with factory prevented misunderstandings

## Common Pitfalls {#common-pitfalls}

### Pitfall 1: Vague Specifications

**What happens:** Manufacturer interprets specs differently, resulting in wrong product.

**Prevention:** Specify exact part numbers. Instead of "Intel i5," write "Intel Core i5-1335U (SRLCY)."

### Pitfall 2: Skipping Prototypes

**What happens:** Design flaw discovered in 1,000-unit production run.

**Prevention:** Always complete EVT → DVT → PVT. Budget 4-8 weeks and $5,000-15,000.

### Pitfall 3: Lowest Price Selection

**What happens:** Quality issues, communication problems, hidden costs.

**Prevention:** Evaluate total cost of ownership. A $20/unit savings means nothing if 5% are defective.

### Pitfall 4: Ignoring Lead Times

**What happens:** Component shortage delays production by 6 weeks.

**Prevention:** Confirm component availability before signing. Build 4-week buffer into timeline.

### Pitfall 5: No Contract Protection

**What happens:** Dispute over quality, no recourse.

**Prevention:** Written contract with specifications, AQL standards, warranty terms, and dispute resolution.

## FAQ {#faq}

### What's the minimum order for custom laptops?

ODM (existing design): 100-300 units
OEM (custom design): 1,000-5,000 units

Some manufacturers offer 50-unit sample orders at 20-30% premium.

### How long does the entire process take?

- First-time project: 12-20 weeks
- Repeat order (same specs): 6-10 weeks
- Full custom OEM design: 6-12 months

### Can I visit the factory?

Yes, and you should for orders over 500 units. Most manufacturers welcome visits. Budget $2,000-4,000 for a China factory visit (flights, hotels, interpreter).

### What certifications do I need?

**Minimum for most markets:**
- USA: FCC Part 15
- EU: CE marking
- Global: RoHS

**Cost:** $5,000-15,000 for full certification suite

### How do I protect my design?

1. Sign NDA before sharing detailed specs
2. Register trademarks in China (where manufacturing happens)
3. Include IP clauses in manufacturing agreement
4. Consider design patents for unique features
